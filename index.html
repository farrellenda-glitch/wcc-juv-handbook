Oversee team registration, mentor ratification, and age-specific issues. Primary point of contact for team leads.

Registration & Subs

Registration Deadline

All players and mentors must be registered by **March 31st** annually. This is mandatory for insurance purposes through the GAA Injury Scheme.

How to Register

Registration is completed via the official club website or the ClubZap app. For assistance, contact the Registrar.

**No Registration = No Play.** Mentors are responsible for ensuring every player on the pitch is registered.

Role of the Mentor

Key Responsibilities

  • Deliver the Player Pathway for your age group.
  • Ensure every child gets equal playing time (U8-U12).
  • Organize coaching sessions (ideally 2 per week).
  • Communicate match details via ClubZap.
  • Uphold the Give Respect / Get Respect initiative.

Vetting & Safeguarding

Every mentor MUST have:
1. Current Garda Vetting
2. Safeguarding 1 Cert
3. Intro to Coaching Cert

The Coaching Process

1

Review & Assess

Analyze the previous season. Identify gaps in technical or physical ability.

2

Define Objectives

Align goals with the Player Pathway. Focus on skill development over winning.

3

Implementation

Execute the 6-week blocks. Ensure maximum participation and enjoyment.

4

Evaluation

Mid-season review with coordinators to track progress against objectives.

Team Grading Policy

We aim to place players in teams where they can compete and develop comfortably.

Level A

Players with high technical and tactical proficiency. High intensity.

Level B

Developing players aiming for competitive play while refining fundamentals.

Level C

Participation-led squads focusing on core skills and social integration.

Note: Movement between teams is possible and encouraged based on player progression.

Pitch & Filming Rules

Filming Protocol

All conditions must be met to film U18 games:

  • Parental permission for all players recorded.
  • Opposing team mentors must agree in advance.
  • Unedited copy must be provided to the committee on request.
  • Fingal/DCC protocols apply for public parks.

Pitch Care

Respect our grounds. No training on match pitches unless authorized. Ensure all gates are locked after the final session.

Match Day Protocols

The "Checklist"

  • First Aid Kit present
  • Correct size sliotars/footballs
  • Respect the Referee
  • Match results texted to board

Returning Results

Results must be returned via SMS or App (depending on code) by 6:00pm on match day to avoid club fines.

FAQ & Safeguarding

Bullying Policy

We adopt the official GAA Code of Behaviour. Any concerns should be directed to the Club Children’s Officer immediately.

Injury Scheme FAQ

Is a player covered if not registered?

No. Unregistered players are not covered and should not be permitted to train or play.

How do I claim?

All claims must be initiated within 60 days of the injury via the Injury Claim form on the club portal.

Player Pathway

Nursery (U4 - U7)

Emphasis on agility, balance, and coordination (ABC) through fun games.

Go Games (U8 - U12)

Participation and skill development. Small-sided games ensure everyone touches the ball.

Competitive (U13+)

Strategic play, tactical awareness, and preparing for adult-level competition.
