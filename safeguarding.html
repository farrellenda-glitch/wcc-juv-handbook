Safeguarding training is mandatory for all coaches, mentors and volunteers who work with children (under 18). It ensures that children participate in Gaelic Games in a safe, respectful and child-centred environment.

The purposed of safeguarding training is as follows:

  • Protect children and young people from harm.
  • Promote a child centred coaching approach.
  • Help adults recognise and respond to child welfare concerns.
  • Ensure compliance with safeguarding legislation and best practice.

There are three levels of Safeguarding training available.


  • Safeguarding 1 is for coaches/mentors.
  • Safeguarding 2 is for Children's Officers.
  • Safeguarding 3 is for Designated Liason Persons.

The Safeguarding 1 course covers the following:

  • The principles of child welfare and protection.
  • The role and responsibilities of coaches and volunteers.
  • Recognising different types of abuse (physical, emotional, sexual, neglect).
  • Responding to concerns and reporting procedures.
  • Codes of behaviour for coaches, parents and young players.
  • Creating a safe and positive environment.

Other details relating to the course:

  • Delivered either face-to-face or online.
  • Typically 3 hours in duration.
  • Valid for 3 years.
