Whitehall Colmcille has a proud and growing tradition in Ladies Gaelic Football and Camogie, providing girls with the opportunity to play, learn and enjoy Gaelic games in a fun, safe and inclusive environment. Our aim is to help every player develop their skills, build confidence and create lifelong friendships while fostering a love of our games, bringing as many girls through to play adult as possible.

Our Ladies Football teams compete under the guidance of Dublin LGFA, while our Camogie teams are affiliated to the Dublin Camogie Board. Coaches, mentors and parents should familiarise themselves with the rules, regulations and player development pathways set out by both governing bodies.

For our youngest age groups, all games are played under the Go Games philosophy, placing the emphasis on participation, enjoyment and player development rather than results. As players progress into competitive grades, all fixtures are governed by the relevant CCC2 Regulations issued by Dublin LGFA and Dublin Camogie.

CCC2 is the Dublin County Board body responsible for the organisation and administration of the older juvenile age grades (Under 13 to Under 16). It plays a central role in ensuring that competitions for children are run in a fair & structured way.

Their key responsibilities are as follows:


  1. • Organises and administers U13–U16 football and hurling leagues, féiles, cups and shield competitions.
  2. • Prepares and manages fixtures, including scheduling, refixtures, and competition calendars.
  3. • Grades teams into appropriate divisions to ensure fair and competitive balance.
  4. • Enforces competition rules, age eligibility regulations, and disciplinary procedures.
  5. • Manages championship structures, including group stages and knockout formats.
  6. • Handles objections, appeals and competition-related disputes.
