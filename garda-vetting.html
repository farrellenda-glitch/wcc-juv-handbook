Under all the associations' rules and to be compliant with the Children and Vulnerable Persons Acts 2012 - 2016 all coaches, mentors and juvenile officers need to be vetted by the National Vetting Bureau of An Garda Síochána.

Garda Vetting is a background check that discloses the following:

  • Criminal convictions (in Ireland).
  • Specified information relating to harm to children or vulnerable persons.
  • Relevant information from other jurisdictions (if applicable).

These do not automatically prevent someone from volunteering — findings are assessed in line with GAA safeguarding policy.


Below are the key steps in the Garda Vetting process with a link to the required documents at the end. (Garda vetting is valid for 3 years)

  • Step 1: Ensure you have completed the GAA Safeguarding 1 course.
  • Step 2: Register for an account on Foireann, the membership and compliance system.
  • Step 3: Inform the Children's Officer that you require vetting, they will submit the application and you will receive an email to complete your application.
  • Step 4: Complete the National Vetting Bureau form sent to your email.
  • Step 5: Your club will verify your identity and upload this to Foireann to complete your submission.
  • Step 6: Your application is reviewed by the National Vetting bureau (usually takes a few weeks).
  • Step 7: The result of the review is disclosed to the GAA and your Children's Officer will be notified of clearance.
  • Step 8: Your Foireann profile will be updated with your vetting status.
